The Team

Engineered from the Ground Up

DermaVision Technologies began as a project in 2022 at the University of California, Irvine, by Dr. Elliot Botvinick. Starting from scratch, inspired students designed a proof-of-concept device, created skin models to mimick bruising, and established basic image processing techniques for analyzing acquired data. Thanks to the BEAMS Lab and support from the Department of Biomedical Engineering (BME), the project continued into the following year as part of the BME Senior Design Program.

Progress was then formally driven by continuing members and, in early 2024, the team was successful in creating a portable prototype and became IRB-approved to perform pre-clinical imaging in collaboration with the UCI Trauma Center. The 2023-24 Senior Design Team went on to earn the Dean's Choice Award at the UCI Engineering Annual Design Review, and won second place in their category at the Stella Zhang New Venture Competition. The project has since expanded to include students across the School of Engineering, and rebranded as DermaVision Technologies.

Kicking off the 2024-25 year, DermaVision Technologies is honored to present their work as Finalists in the BMES 2024 Student Design Competition, at the BMES Annual Meeting on October 25th.

Our diverse team of current UCI students and recent alumni are working passionately towards a minimum viable product, and continue gathering data in conjunction with the UCI Trauma Center.
